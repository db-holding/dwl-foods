DWL's headquarters is in Londrina, Brazil, south of the country which thanks to the climate, latitude, longitude and fertile land was considered the world’s agricultural capital, a privileged location to import and export. The region’s volcanic soil that helps to provide high quality coffee.

    Region Highlights

  • SUSTAINABILITY: In Londrina - Paraná there are dozens of institutes and universities for coffee’s study and development.
  • ENVIRONMENTAL AND SOCIAL DEVELOPMENT: There are tourist routes for tasting the drink, named: Coffee Route. A project that allows groups of tourists a historical, cultural, and pleasant tour that allows them to taste different coffee types.
  • LEADER: Brazil is the world’s largest coffee producer and is among the three largest suppliers of Arabica and Robusta varieties in the world.
  • COMMITMENT: With increasing export volume and working sustainably. Assessing the social and financial impact of their activities on society and preserving natural resources.
  • TECHNOLOGY: From the planting process until exporting. Working since production, DWL uses crop automation, mechanized harvest implementation. This reduces harvest time by 60% and allows you to have more coffee to dry at once. This guarantees a premium quality to the coffee bean.

Expertise

DWL operates in a coordinated manner from production and the relationship with the rural producer, storage, classification, logistics, to the marketing and exportation of the product, aiming at the growth and sustainability of the links in the supply chain.

Brazilian coffee growers have been responsible for 1/3 of the world’s production, which has placed the country as the largest coffee producer in the world for the past century. Brazil has also been the largest product exporter in the world. As the local production continues to grow, better and larger business opportunities are available.

Our trade experience and our passion for coffee production, allows us to curate several types of coffee beans (Catuai, Kona, Bourbon and Arabic) exploring the world’s demand for the most consumed beverage in the globe.
